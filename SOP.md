# **Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) || Geeks of Gurukul**

**Organization:** Skillscan Edtech India Pvt. Ltd. (*Known as* **“Geeks of Gurukul”**)

**1. Purpose:**

The purpose of this SOP is to establish clear and professional guidelines for all employees regarding dress code, attendance, conduct, leaves and other office-related policies. It aims to create a respectful, productive, and organized environment at Geeks of Gurukul.

**2. Scope:**

This SOP applies to all employees *(which includes SDE & Professors, Operation Manager, HoI, Interns and other employees of different dept.)* at Geeks of Gurukul, including those engaged in administrative roles, academic staff, support staff etc.

**3.1 Dress Code Policy**

**Mandatory Dress Code**

All employees must strictly adhere to the following dress code during working hours:

- **White formal shirt with collar**
- **Black Pant with Belt**
- **Black formal blazer**
- **Formal shoes**

Casual attire is **strictly prohibited** unless specifically approved by Management for official events.

### **Dress Code Violation – Disciplinary Process (For All Professors)**

Non-compliance with the prescribed dress code will result in **progressive disciplinary action**, applicable to **all Professors**, and will be recorded in HR records:

1. **1st Instance**
    - **First warning via email**
    - Entry recorded in HR records
2. **2nd Instance**
    - **Second warning via email**
    - Entry recorded in HR records
3. **3rd Instance**
    - **Third warning via email**
    - **10% fine deducted from one day’s gross salary**
4. **4th Instance**
    - **Fourth warning via email**
    - **20% fine deducted from one day’s gross salary**
5. **5th Instance and Subsequent Violations**
    - **Fifth warning via email**
    - **30% fine deducted from one day’s gross salary**
    - Further disciplinary action may be initiated at the Management’s discretion.

---

### **Important Notes**

- Penalties will be applied **per day of non-compliance**.
- Repeated violations may adversely impact **performance reviews and disciplinary records**.
- The Management reserves the right to take **strict disciplinary action** in cases of habitual non-compliance.

**3.2 Attendance Timing:**

- **General Attendance Requirements:**
    - All faculty members are required to post their geotag attendance in their respective Attendance Channel  within the office premises **at the official office hours**, as per the schedule shared by the HR department.
    
    A **grace period of 2 minutes** will be allowed. Any attendance marked beyond this will be considered late.
    
- **Late Arrival Procedure:**
    - All faculty members are required to post their attendance in their respective college groups within the office premises at the official office hours, as per the schedule shared by the HR department.
    - A **grace period of 2 minutes** will be allowed. Any attendance marked beyond this will be considered late.
    - If an employee is unable to post their attendance at office time, they must submit a separate attendance post with a valid reason explaining the delay.
    
    **Late Arrivals Policy:**
    
    - **Repeated Late Arrivals:** If an employee arrives late repeatedly (more than 3 instances in a month), disciplinary actions will be initiated by the HR department, which may include:
        - **Half-day pay cut** – 4th instance
        - **Full-day pay cut** – 5th instance
        - **2 days’ pay cut** – 6th instance and onwards
    
    **Additional Rules & Penalties:**
    
    - If any employee forgets to upload their attendance (Clock-in and Clock-out), a **fine of Rs. 500** will be charged.
    - A photo must be uploaded on Slack Attendance Channel within **30 minutes of Clock-in**. Failure to do so more than **2**  **instance in a month without proper reason** will result in a **fine of Rs. 100** for each instance thereafter.
    - Clock-in and Clock-out photos must be taken inside the college premises. Attendance outside requires prior permission from your reporting manager.
- **Consequences for Non-Compliance:**
    - Employees who continuously fail to adhere to attendance policies will face progressive disciplinary actions, including verbal and written warnings and, in severe cases, pay cuts or suspension or termination.

**3.3 Office Premises and Hours:**

- **Presence Within Office Premises:**
    - All employees have to be present within the office premises during working hours, unless permission for an exception is granted.
    - Employees are allowed to leave the premises **only** during the designated lunch break. Any request to leave outside this time must be approved by the Reporting Manager.
    - If Employees are going out of college premises during the lunch break, they need to mention the same in the attendance channel in the thread of where they posted their clock in as below:

**Msg:** “Going for Lunch”

**Msg:** “Returned to college after Lunch”

**Note: DO NOT** make new message to send “Going For Lunch” Message

- **Reporting Issues:**
    - Employees must report any issues or concerns related to work or office environment to their respective Reporting Managers through mail or slack even after discussing physically. All issues must be addressed professionally and in a timely manner.

**3.4 Office Work and Conduct:**

- **Focus on Office Responsibilities:**
    - During office hours, employees must remain focused on official tasks, such as preparing lecture notes, assignments, or any other work-related duties.
    - Employees should refrain from engaging in any personal or non-work-related activities within the office premises.
- **Clear Boundaries Between Students and Faculty:**
    - Faculty members must maintain professional boundaries with students, ensuring no personal or inappropriate engagements. Any violation of these boundaries will be taken seriously and disciplinary action will follow.

**3.5 Professionalism with College Administration:**

- **Communication with College Administration:**
    - Employees must maintain professionalism when interacting with the college administration. Any official information or updates should be shared with the **Head of Institute** (HoI) to ensure proper communication channels are followed.
- **Reporting Negative Behaviour:**
    - Employees are prohibited from spreading negativity about the organization. This includes criticizing the organization’s policies, operations, or personnel, and especially spreading negativity about new faculty members.
    - Any instances of office politics or rumours must be reported directly to HR and the respective Reporting Managers for immediate action.

**3.6 Availability during Office Hours:**

- **Communication Expectations:**
    - During office hours, all faculty members must be available for communication via phone calls, Slack and emails. This ensures effective communication within the team and with students.
    - If any employee is unable to join a scheduled Google Meet, he/she must inform **at least 10 minutes in advance** in their respective college channel on Slack by tagging the **reporting manager and HR**.
    - If he/she is absent in the meeting without informing in the channel and Reporting Manager then  **disciplinary action will be taken**.
- **Strict Action for Ignored Communication:**
    - If an employee continuously ignores calls, messages over Slack or emails, they will be subject to strong disciplinary action. This includes formal warnings or pay deductions depending on the severity of the situation.

**3.7 Leave Policy for SDE & Professor & HoI’s**

- **Total 12 Paid Leave** provided in **1 Year** that starts from **date of joining**.
- They can avail **1 Paid Leave in 1 Month** .

**NOTE :** More than 1 Leave will be considered as Un-Paid Leave

**Leave Procedure**

**1. Casual Leave**

• The employee must obtain permission from the Head of Institute (HoI) at least 18 hours in advance, either through a phone call or Slack.

• After receiving approval from the HOI, the employee Immediately  will send an email to HR  ([hr@geeksofgurukul.com](mailto:hr@geeksofgurukul.com)), and Keep CC HoI, Academic Lead, CTO Sir ([cto@geeksofgurukul.com](mailto:cto@geeksofgurukul.com)),COO Sir ( [coo@geeksofgurukul.com](mailto:coo@geeksofgurukul.com)  before 16 Hour of next day( that is before 6pm. else the leave will not  be approved

• It is mandatory to obtain prior approval for the leave; otherwise, the leave will not be considered a paid leave, and the employee’s salary will be subject to deduction accordingly.

• If any employee avails leave, without sending an email to HR ([hr@geeksofgurukul.com](mailto:hr@geeksofgurukul.com)), and Keep CC HoI, Academic Lead [cto@geeksofgurukul.com](mailto:cto@geeksofgurukul.com), [coo@geeksofgurukul.com](mailto:coo@geeksofgurukul.com)  **then it will be Consider as Leave without Pay and Penalty of Rs.500**

**2. Emergency Leave**

- In case of an emergency, the employee must first **inform their reporting manager via call**. If the reporting manager is not available, the employee must **inform HR via call**.
- After recovering from the emergency, the employee must **send a**  emergency **leave  email to HR at hr@geeksofgurukul.com**, keeping **CC to the HoI, cto@geeksofgurukul.com, and coo@geeksofgurukul.com**, along with **valid proof of the emergency**.
- If the proof is not provided, found invalid, or if the employee fails to send the email after recovering, the leave will be considered **unpaid**, and a **fine of Rs. 500** will be applicable.

**3. College Employee Leave Criteria**

- Only **one professor from each college** shall be permitted to take leave on the same day. In case of multiple requests, the **Head of Institute (HoI)** will approve leave based on **operational needs** and **who applied first.**
- If Head of Institute (HoI) does not grant leave approval to an employee, and the employee still proceeds to take leave without authorization, it shall be considered a **two-days unpaid leave** (2 days) for every **one day of unauthorized absence** as a disciplinary measure. In case of a medical emergency, the leave must be **verified** first—**if not verified,** it will also be treated as **two days of unpaid leave per day**.
- There are no paid leaves during the notice period of an employee until and unless it is an medical emergency.

**3.8 Leave Policy for Operation Manager & Associate.**

- **Total 12 Paid Leave** provided in **1 Year** that starts from **date of joining**.
- They can avail **1 Paid Leave in 1 Month** .

**NOTE :** More than 1 Leave will be considered as Un-Paid Leave

**Leave Procedure**

**1. Casual Leave**

• The employee must submit a application viva mail to the Head of Institute (HOI) at least 24 hours in advance.

• **After receiving approval from the HOI,** the employee will send an email to HR ([hr@geeksofgurukul.com](mailto:hr@geeksofgurukul.com)), and **Keep CC HoI, [cto@geeksofgurukul.com](mailto:cto@geeksofgurukul.com), [coo@geeksofgurukul.com](mailto:coo@geeksofgurukul.com)**  before 15 Hour of next day( that is before 6pm. else the leave will not  be approved

• It is mandatory to obtain prior approval for the leave; otherwise, the leave will not be considered a paid leave, and the employee’s salary will be subject to deduction accordingly.

**2. Emergency Leave**

In case of an emergency, the employee must first send a leave request via email to HR at [hr@geeksofgurukul.com](mailto:hr@geeksofgurukul.com) , and **keep CC to the HoI, [cto@geeksofgurukul.com](mailto:cto@geeksofgurukul.com) , and [coo@geeksofgurukul.com](mailto:coo@geeksofgurukul.com) .**

- After the emergency, the employee **must reply in the same email thread with valid proof** of the emergency. If the proof is not provided or found to be invalid, the leave will be considered as unpaid leave.

• Failure to follow this procedure or submit sufficient justification may result in the leave being treated as unpaid, leading to a salary deduction

**NOTE :** If the above is not followed then it will be considered as **Un-Paid Leave.**

**3.9 Work from Home (WFH) Policy for Operation Manager & Associate.**

- **Emergency Work from Home:**
    
    Operation Manager (OM) may be permitted to work from home in case of emergencies, at the discretion of the company's management. However, evidence (such as a medical certificate or other documentation) must be provided to support the request.
    
    If an employee intends to work from home due to an emergency, they must obtain approval from the HoI. The HoI will assign specific tasks, and if the assigned tasks are not completed by the end of the day, the day will be marked as leave.
    
1. **Unavailability During WFH:**
    
    If an employee is unable to work during their WFH hours, then **that day will be considered as leave without Pay.**
    
2. **Task Discussion and Submission:**
    
    Employees must discuss the assigned task with the Head of Institution (HoI) one day before in advance. **All tasks must be submitted by 9:00 PM on the day of WFH.**
    
3. **Meeting Attendance Requirement:**
    
    During the WFH period, **each employee is required to attend at least one Ops meeting.**
    
    If the employee is unable to attend both scheduled meetings, prior permission must be obtained from the HoI via Slack or Call.
    
- **Notification Requirement:**
    - Operation Manager (OM) must inform HR and their Reporting Manager at least 15 hours before the start of the next day (i.e., before 6:00 PM on the previous day)**.**
- **WFH Pay Structure:**
    - Operation Managers (OM) who are allowed to work from home will receive **half-day pay** for the day.
    
    **3.10 General Mandatory Protocols for Operation Manager (OM)/Interns**
    
    1. **Meeting Communication:**
        - Candidates must inform the HoI at least **10 minutes in advance via call or Slack** if they are **unable to join the scheduled Ops meeting .**
    2. **Minutes of Meeting (MoM):**
        - It is mandatory to mention the presence or absence of the Operations Manager in the MoM of the Ops meeting.
    3. **Dashboard Updates:**
        - Every Operations Manager must regularly and accurately update their respective dashboard channel to ensure proper task tracking and transparency.

**3.10 Prohibition of Smoking and Chewing Tobacco:**

- **Strict No-Smoking and No-Tobacco Policy:**
    - Smoking and the use of chewing tobacco are strictly prohibited within the office premises. Violation of this policy will result in disciplinary action, including possible suspension.

**3.11 Harassment-Free Workplace:**

- **Zero Tolerance for Harassment:**
    - All employees and faculty must maintain a respectful, harassment-free work environment. Any form of sexual harassment or inappropriate behaviour will not be tolerated.
- **Reporting Violations:**
    - Employees must immediately report any harassment incidents to HR. The organization will investigate all complaints promptly and fairly.
- **Consequences of Harassment:**
    - Employees found guilty of sexual harassment or other inappropriate conduct will face strict disciplinary actions, which may include suspension or termination based on the severity of the violation.

**3.12 Probation & Notice Period**

- All new employees will be on a **6-month probation period** from their date of joining. During this period, the **notice period will be either 7 days or 30 days**, as decided by the company.
- The company reserves the right to extend this period at its discretion. During the probationary period, your employment may be terminated with a notice period of 7 or 30 days in case of performance-related issues.

**Full and Final (FNF) Settlement Timeline**

- As per company policy, the Full and Final (FNF) settlement process shall be completed **within a maximum of 45 calendar days** from the employee’s last working day.
- This timeline includes the calculation and clearance of all pending dues such as salary, reimbursements, leave encashment, and any other applicable payments.
- The settlement will be aligned with the company’s payroll cycle, and employees can expect disbursement of their dues **between the 15th and 20th of the following month**, subject to the 45-day timeline.

**3.13 Performance-Based Termination Policy for SDEs & Professors:**

To maintain the highest standards of education delivery at Geeks of Gurukul:

- If an SDE & Professor receives a **student rating below 4.2** for **three consecutive weeks**, they will receive **three formal warnings** (one after each instance).
- If there is **no improvement in the student rating** post these warnings, the employee **may be terminated with a 7-day notice period**.
- The decision will be jointly taken by the HoI and HR after reviewing performance, student feedback, and any corrective measures taken by the employee.

**4. Responsibility:**

- **Employees:**
    - All employees are responsible for following the policies outlined in this SOP, including attendance, dress code, communication, and conduct.
- **HR Department:**
    - The HR department is responsible for enforcing this SOP, handling disciplinary actions, and providing guidance regarding attendance, communication, and conduct issues.
- **Reporting Managers:**
    - Reporting Managers are responsible for ensuring that all employees adhere to the SOP. They are the first point of contact for any concerns regarding attendance, performance, or conduct.

**5. Review and Revision:**

This SOP will be reviewed quarterly by the HR department and the administration to ensure it remains up-to-date and effective. Updates will be made as needed based on feedback, changes in policies, or legal requirements.

**6. Effective Date:**

This SOP will be effective as of: **1st April 2025**

By adhering to the guidelines established in this SOP, Geeks of Gurukul seeks to create a professional and respectful environment that enhances productivity, fosters positive relationships, and ensures the well-being of all employees, faculty, and students
